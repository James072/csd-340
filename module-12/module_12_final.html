Jay's Hobbies

Hip Hop Music

Throughout my life, I've been exposed to different genres of music from a very young age -
classical, jazz, gospel, opera - you name it. But from the age of 12, Hip Hop grabbed a hold of me and never let go! I threw my hat into the ring and became an emcee (M-C), freestyling about random stuff until age 15, when I realized I needed to start writing rhymes down on paper. I named myself Jay Money and 2 years later, I met my long-time producer and good friend, Todd "DJ Suicide" Reese. I started out rapping the intros to his mixtapes, and the rest is history! The three covers pictured above are projects that I was directly involved with. The first was with my crew, Da Basement Society, during the 90's, and later, I released a solo mixtape and a solo album. I guess you could say this has been a little more than a hobby.
Check out discography!

Collecting Watches

Hi! My name is James and I'm a watch collecting addict! To say that I love watches is an understatement. When I was a kid, my grandfather used to always have me set the time on his new watches, and then tell me that "every man should have a good watch!" Then, he and my grandmother gave me a chunky, stainless-steel Timex for my 13th birthday. My addiction started off small, buying cheap watches from bootleg spots and boosters (a shoplifter who operates in the streets!). But when the first rechargeable watches hit the scene, I had to have one, so I started spending more money. I was also a member of an online watch club that sent me a new watch every month, but I canceled that, as it was starting to get out of hand. In 2011, I discovered Invicta watches and got sprung again! I currently have enough watches to wear a different watch everyday for two months without repeating. Watches are my drugs!

Creative Writing

My natural talent is my skill to write. I started out writing short stories as a kid, and then in junior high, I served as sports editor of my school's newspaper. My ability to write is what lead me into writing rap lyrics. When we ran our independent label during the 90's, I wrote all of the bios for artist press kits. A goal of mine is to have a published work, and I'm currently working on a book titled "A Father's Right," which chronicles the events of the 2-year court dispute in which I gained custody of my son (there's a movie deal in this, I know it!).

My Experience

As for the world of Information Technology, I haven't gone as far as acquiring an actual job, yet.
So, in this section, I briefly show my journey thus far.

Shift Manager


From 2003 until 2018 (13 years), I worked for an employer that owned two convenience store/gas stations directly across the street from each other in north Omaha - BJ's and Ames Avenue. In 2006, I was promoted to manager over the night shift, which would have been easy had it not been for the surrounding elements of this particular environment. During my time there, I witnessed shootings, fights, a man murdered in the store during the day shift....I probably would have quit earlier, but the pay was good and allowed me to fight for custody of my son, who has special needs. In 2018, I couldn't stand it anymore, so I quit and registered for school.

Back To School


I left the well-paying, dead-end job and registered for school. I had a small amount of experience with HTML, but that's it. When registering, I was asked what I wanted to do. My answer was, "I want to learn how to program." While at Metro, I took Java 1 & 2, Python 1 & 2, 2 SQL courses, database design and data center operations, XML, Web Page Development, just to name a few. To make ends meet, I worked part-time as a car detailer for Enterprise Rent-A-Car, and later worked at the bookstore/coffee shop on the Metro campus (not work study) until May of this year. I earned my associate degree in Information Technology, Programming for Database and Web Design in November of 2021 (took a summer off to work), and participated in the commencement ceremony back in May of 2022.

The Next Stage

When researching IT jobs, many employers are requiring candidates to have a bachelors degree, according to the job postings. I decided to take it to the next level and transferred to Bellevue University. I entered into the accelerated cohort because I felt that being older, I didn't have time to pursue this degree over a four- year period; a year and a half sounded more feasible. It's been hard, with trying to juggle a special-need son, going back to full-time work, and the death of close family members. I refuse to stop, regardless to having a couple of bad quarters. You have to pick yourself up and continue to strive towards accomplishing the goal.
